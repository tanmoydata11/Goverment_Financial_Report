## Financial Metrics & KPIs

### Core Financial Metrics

**1. Gross Sales (Revenue)**
- **Calculation:** SUM(Gross Sales)
- **Definition:** Total sales value before any discounts
- **Formula:** Units Sold × Sale Price (theoretical)
- **Typical Value:** $3,000,000+ for full year
- **Insight:** Top-line revenue indicator
- **Trend:** Monthly variation reflects seasonality

**2. Total Discounts**
- **Calculation:** SUM(Discounts)
- **Definition:** Total discount amount given to customers
- **Insight:** Shows discount strategy generosity
- **Typical:** $100,000-$300,000 annually
- **Impact:** Directly reduces net sales
- **Optimization:** Opportunity to reduce aggressive discounting

**3. Net Sales (After Discounts)**
- **Calculation:** SUM(Sales) or Gross Sales - Discounts
- **Definition:** Actual revenue after all discounts applied
- **Formula:** SUM(Sales) column
- **Insight:** True revenue available for profit
- **Range:** 90-100% of Gross Sales depending on discount aggressiveness
- **Business Use:** Core revenue for margin calculation

**4. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**
- **Calculation:** SUM(COGS)
- **Definition:** Direct cost to manufacture products sold
- **Formula:** Units Sold × Manufacturing Price (theoretical)
- **Typical Value:** $1,000,000-$1,500,000
- **Insight:** Production cost structure
- **Margin:** COGS typically 40-60% of Net Sales

**5. Total Profit**
- **Calculation:** SUM(Profit) or Net Sales - COGS
- **Definition:** Net profit after all costs
- **Insight:** Bottom-line profitability
- **Typical Value:** $1,000,000+
- **Target:** 30-40% profit margin in this industry
- **Optimization:** Largest opportunity for improvement

**6. Profit Margin %**
- **Calculation:** (Total Profit / Net Sales) × 100
- **Definition:** Percentage return on sales
- **Typical Range:** 30-40%
- **Performance:** Varies significantly by segment and country
- **Insight:** Operational efficiency metric
- **Benchmark:** Compare against industry standards

### Advanced Financial Metrics

**7. Gross Profit Margin %**
- **Calculation:** ((Gross Sales - COGS) / Gross Sales) × 100
- **Insight:** Manufacturing efficiency
- **Typical:** 50-60% before discounts

**8. Discount Rate %**
- **Calculation:** (Total Discounts / Gross Sales) × 100
- **Insight:** Discount strategy aggressiveness
- **Typical:** 5-15% of gross sales
- **Optimization:** Opportunity if rate is high

**9. Average Transaction Value**
- **Calculation:** Net Sales / Count of Transactions
- **Insight:** Customer basket size
- **Typical:** $4,000-$6,000 per transaction

**10. Cost Per Unit**
- **Calculation:** COGS / Units Sold
- **Insight:** Manufacturing efficiency
- **Typical:** $3-$20 depending on product

#### **DAX Measures**

**Basic Aggregations:**
```DAX
Total Gross Sales = SUM(FinancialData[Gross Sales])
Total Discounts = SUM(FinancialData[Discounts])
Total Net Sales = SUM(FinancialData[Sales])
Total COGS = SUM(FinancialData[COGS])
Total Profit = SUM(FinancialData[Profit])
Total Units = SUM(FinancialData[Units Sold])
Order Count = COUNTA(FinancialData[Country])
```

**Percentage Measures:**
```DAX
Profit Margin % = DIVIDE([Total Profit], [Total Net Sales], 0) * 100
Discount Rate % = DIVIDE([Total Discounts], [Total Gross Sales], 0) * 100
Gross Margin % = DIVIDE([Total Gross Sales] - [Total COGS], 
                         [Total Gross Sales], 0) * 100
```

**Average Measures:**
```DAX
Avg Order Value = DIVIDE([Total Net Sales], [Order Count], 0)
Avg Profit per Order = DIVIDE([Total Profit], [Order Count], 0)
Avg Units per Order = DIVIDE([Total Units], [Order Count], 0)
```

**Conditional Measures:**
```DAX
Profitable Orders = CALCULATE([Order Count], [Profit] > 0)
Loss Orders = CALCULATE([Order Count], [Profit] < 0)
High Margin Products = CALCULATE([Order Count], [Profit Margin %] > 40)
```

## Recommendations

### 1. Discount Strategy Optimization

**Finding:** Aggressive discounting erodes margins significantly

**Actions:**
- **Immediate:** Audit all transactions with >10% discount
- **Short-term:** Reduce average discount rate from current to <10%
- **Strategy:** Implement discount approval workflow
- **Timeline:** 30-60 days for review, 90 days for implementation

**Expected Impact:** 
- 2-3% overall profit margin improvement
- $60K-$90K additional annual profit on $3M revenue

**Implementation:**
- Government segment: Maintain baseline discounts, improve efficiency
- Midmarket: Selective discounting only for high-value deals
- Channel Partners: Structured discount bands based on volume

### 2. Segment-Specific Strategies

#### **Government Segment**
- **Challenge:** Price-sensitive, requires discounts
- **Opportunity:** Efficiency and volume optimization
- **Action:** 
  - Standardize products to reduce SKU proliferation
  - Volume-based discounts instead of transaction-based
  - Streamline order processing for cost reduction
- **Target:** Maintain volume, improve margins to 35-40%

#### **Midmarket Segment**
- **Opportunity:** Highest profit potential
- **Action:**
  - Implement value-based selling (not discount-based)
  - Develop premium product tiers
  - Focus on customer success stories
- **Target:** Grow segment by 20%, maintain 40%+ margins

#### **Channel Partners Segment**
- **Challenge:** Wholesale margins require careful management
- **Action:**
  - Tiered discount structure based on volume commitments

## 📊 Appendix

### Financial Metric Definitions

**Gross Sales:** Total revenue before any deductions (Units × Sale Price)

**Discounts:** Total discount amount applied to reduce sale price

**Net Sales (Sales):** Revenue after discount application (Gross Sales - Discounts)

**Manufacturing Price:** Cost per unit to manufacture the product

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold):** Total manufacturing cost (Units × Manufacturing Price, approximately)

**Profit:** Net profit after all costs (Net Sales - COGS)

**Profit Margin %:** (Profit / Net Sales) × 100, indicates profitability percentage

**Units Sold:** Quantity of units in each transaction

**Discount Band:** Category of discount level (None, Small, Medium, Large)

### Abbreviations Used

- **COGS:** Cost of Goods Sold
- **KPI:** Key Performance Indicator
- **P&L:** Profit & Loss
- **ROI:** Return on Investment
- **YoY:** Year-over-Year
- **MoM:** Month-over-Month
- **B2G:** Business-to-Government
- **SKU:** Stock Keeping Unit

### Segment Definitions

**Government:** B2G sales to government agencies and institutions
- Stable, volume-based purchasing
- Often price-sensitive
- Compliance and budget-driven
- Predictable patterns

**Midmarket:** Mid-sized business sales
- Growth-focused segment
- Quality and value-conscious
- Flexible budget and negotiable pricing
- Variable buying patterns

**Channel Partners:** Resellers, distributors, retailers
- Wholesale segment with discount structures
- Bulk purchasing patterns
- Margin-dependent operations
- Contractual relationships
